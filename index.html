About PSCGT
PSG College of Technology (PSG CT), founded in 1951 by PSG & Sons’ Charities, stands as an eminent institution in the field of education. It is an ISO 9001-2015 certified autonomous institution affiliated to Anna University, Chennai. The college offers a diverse array of academic programs with 21 undergraduate and 24 postgraduate programs covering Engineering and Technology, Computer Applications, Management Sciences, Basic and Applied Sciences. Distinguished by its commitment to innovation and research, PSG CT houses several cutting-edge Centers of Excellence funded by MHRD, DST and other agencies. TIFAC Core, Advanced Machine Tool Research Centre, Virtual Instrumentation Centre, Centre of Excellence for Industrial and Home Textiles, Centre for Nano-technology, Centre for Robotics, Centre for Excellence in Artificial Intelligence and Software, Danfoss Centre of Excellence in Climate and Energy, and Centre of Excellence for Welding Engineering and Technology are a few to mention. In addition to its academic pursuits, PSG CT has a strong industrial presence. The PSG Industrial Institute operates manufacturing units for castings, machine tools, pumps, motors and technical textiles. PSG CT's collaboration with industry, research institutes, alumni, and entrepreneurs underscores its commitment to holistic education, innovation, and industry relevance.

About Department of Automobile Engineering

The department was established with an undergraduate programme in Automobile engineering in 1999.

  • B.E-Automobile Engineering (4 Years)
  • M.E-Automotive Engineering (2 Years)
  • Ph.D-Automobile Engineering : Full-time and Part-time

The faculty of the department has published 200+ technical papers in various National and International conferences and journals from 1999. The department is well equipped with laboratories like PACE Lab, Vehicle Servicing Lab, PSG Motorsports Lab, Electric Hybrid Vehicle Lab, Engine Trouble Shooting Lab, Vehicle Performance and Characteristic Lab, Two & Three Wheeler Lab, Automotive Steering System Lab, Automotive Simulation Lab, Automotive Electronics Lab, CAD & CAE Lab, Fuel Cell Lab, Centre for Automotive Research and Testing Lab & Centre of Excellence - Industrial & Home Textiles lab. The software packages available in the department include Pro-E, Solidworks, Solid edge, NX, Siemens, CATIA, Unigraphics, ANSYS, ADAMS, LSDYNA for the faculty and aspiring students to pursue their research and project work. The upgraded facilities in the department include sensors, CVT, Automatic Transmission, etc. The department offers consultancy services in Chassis Design, Engine Design, FEA analysis, and CFD simulations. In the near future, the department aims to start Fuel Testing Laboratory, Advanced Automotive Electronics Laboratory, and a Research center for Alternate Fuels.

The department maintains a close liaison with a number of Universities and Industries in India and abroad through faculty research and collaborative projects.

ABOUT CU-ICAR
Founded in 2007, the Clemson University International Center for Automotive Research (CU-ICAR) is an advanced-technology research campus where education, research, and economic development collaborate to create a global venue for the automotive industry. CU-ICAR is home to the nation’s only graduate Department of Automotive Engineering. Here, over 200 Clemson University students are pursuing Master of Science and/or Ph.D. degrees in Automotive Engineering. Thanks to the work of our world-class faculty, our graduate students learn in an innovative research-and-educational program that focuses on the vehicle and its infrastructure from a systems-integration perspective. Our entire program is devoted to the discipline of automotive engineering.
ABOUT THE CONFERENCE
The International Conference on Emerging Technologies in Transportation for Sustainable Future is a global event aimed at bringing together researchers, scholars, and professionals to explore the latest advancements in transportation technologies and their role in fostering sustainability. This conference serves as a dynamic platform for unveiling groundbreaking research, pioneering concepts, and forward-looking insights into the future of sustainable transportation. With a focus on emerging technologies in transportation and their potential to address social, environmental, and industrial challenges, the conference aims to catalyze innovative solutions and shape the trajectory of the sustainable transportation landscape.
SCOPE OF THE CONFERENCE
This conference will delve into the transformative potential of emerging technologies in transportation to drive a sustainable future. Discussions will center on advancements such as electric and autonomous vehicles, smart mobility systems, and sustainable fuels. The focus will be on how these innovations can reduce carbon emissions, enhance efficiency, and promote equitable access to transportation. Experts will explore policy implications, infrastructure requirements, and the role of public-private partnerships in realizing a more sustainable and inclusive transportation landscape.

THEMATIC SESSIONS

Electric and Autonomous Vehicles

Explore the latest advancements in electric and autonomous vehicle technologies, their impact on transportation sustainability, and future trends in this rapidly evolving field.

Smart Infrastructure and Mobility Solutions

Discuss innovative solutions and technologies for smart infrastructure, including intelligent transportation systems, traffic management, and sustainable urban mobility planning.

Alternative Fuels and Hybrid Sources

Delve into the development and adoption of alternative fuels such as hydrogen, biofuels, and renewable energy sources to reduce the carbon footprint of transportation.

Artificial Intelligence

Examine the role of data analytics, machine learning, and AI in optimizing transportation systems, enhancing efficiency, and improving overall sustainability.

Urban Air Mobility and Future of Flight

Focus on emerging trends in urban air mobility, including eVTOLs (electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft), drones, and their potential impact on sustainable transportation.

Intelligent Transportation Systems

Advances focusing on innovations in smart mobility, connected vehicles, and infrastructure technologies highlighting the role of ITS in enhancing transportation efficiency, safety, and sustainability for future smart cities.

Cybersecurity in Vehicles

Challenges and solutions in vehicle cybersecurity, covering secure communication, data protection, and threat mitigation for connected and autonomous vehicles

Smart Transport Networks

Development of Smart Transport Networks, emphasizing connected infrastructure & vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communication showcasing and supporting the future of connected transportation.

Author Guidelines

ICETTS 2025 invites full-length original research contributions from engineering professionals from industries, R&D organizations, academic institutions, government departments, and research scholars from across the world.

  • The conference proceedings will be published in Scopus indexed Springer book series.
  • Full-length original research contributions and review articles as per the given paper format shall be submitted.
  • The manuscript should not have been published in any journals/magazines or conference proceedings and must not be under review in any of them.
  • The manuscript should contain the name of the corresponding author with an email ID and the affiliation of all authors.
  • ICETTS 2025 will not publish any paper that has not been registered.
  • If a complaint regarding the authorship of a paper is received and found to be valid, the paper will be immediately withdrawn from publication.
  • Submit the manuscripts through the "Register" button.
  • Submitted research work must be original in content and free from plagiarism.
  • Refer to the brochure for more information on formatting the abstract and paper.
